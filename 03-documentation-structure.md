# **Documentation structure**

This page outlines the Building Blocks of the Green Deal Data Space Rulebook.

This Rulebook is structured based on the [building blocks defined in the](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071251457/Data+Spaces+Blueprint+v2.0+-+Home) [<span class="underline">Data Spaces Blueprint 3.0 by the Data Spaces Support Centre,</span>](https://blueprint.dssc.eu/) which in turn is based on the [<span class="underline">Open DEI project.</span>](https://design-principles-for-data-spaces.org/)

The GDDS Rulebook adopts a building block approach to ensure modularity, scalability, and interoperability. Each building block represents a distinct set of capabilities required for the functioning of the data space, while allowing flexibility in implementation across different use cases and participants. This approach enables the GDDS to align with European data space standards while supporting incremental development and integration of additional functionalities over time.

The Figure below describes the following building blocks:  
<span class="underline">1. [Business and Organisational Building Blocks](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071252613)</span>: These address key capabilities needed in three sub-areas:

  - > [<span class="underline">Business</span>](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071252740): The business model of the GDDS and its participants, the development of use cases, the role of intermediaries and operators of services and the concept of a data space offering.

  - > [<span class="underline">Governance</span>](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071253634): The organisational form of the GDDS initiative, the governance processes and the management of participants.

  - > [<span class="underline">Legal</span>](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071253899): The required contractual framework between participants and regulatory compliance.

<span class="underline">2. [Technical Building Blocks](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071254703)</span>: These address the key capabilities on a technical level. They are also subdivided into three categories:

  - > [<span class="underline">Data Interoperability</span>](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071255207): Specifying domain models/semantics, technical interfaces for data exchange and approaches for provenance & traceability.

  - > [<span class="underline">Data Sovereignty & Trust</span>](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071255699): Enabling the identification of participants, verifying compliance and specifying and enforcing data access and usage policies.

  - > [<span class="underline">Data Value Creation Enablers</span>](https://dssc.eu/space/BVE2/1071256290): Describing and publishing of data products and making them findable for others (e.g. in a catalogue), as well as introducing additional services for value-creation in your data space.
